${_("Article 5 (Rights, Duties and Execution Methods of Information Subjects)")}

  1. ① Regarding The homepage, the information subject can exercise the right related with personal information protection of the following items at any time.
    • 1. Request of viewing personal information
      The reading of personal information file of The homepage can be demanded according to Article 35 of "Personal Information Protection Act". However, pursuant to Article 35, Item 4 of the same Act, the following items may restrict the reading.
      • Reading is prohibited or restricted by law.
      • If there is a risk of harming another person's life or body or there is a risk of unjustly infringing the property or other interests of another person.
      • If a public institution causes serious disruption in the performance of any of the following businesses:
        • Tax levy collection or refund business
        • Business on grading or selecting enrollees at each level of schools in accordance with (The Law on Primary and Secondary Education) and (Higher Education Act), lifelong education facilities under (the Lifelong Education Act), and higher education institutions established by other laws
        • Business on the examination of academic ability and employment, qualification examination
        • Business on ongoing assessment or judgment on compensation and benefit calculation, etc.
        • Audit and investigation business in progress under other laws
    • 2. Request of personal information correction and deletion
      The correction and deletion of personal information file of the homepage can be requested in accordance with Article 36 of "Personal Information Protection Act". However, in cases where the personal information is specified as the object of collection under other laws, it can not be requested to be deleted.
    • 3. Request to stop processing personal information
      According to Article 37 of "Personal Information Protection Act", the suspension of processing personal information file of The homepage can be demanded. However, when requesting suspension of personal information processing, the request for suspension of processing may be rejected pursuant to Article 37, Item 2 of the Act.
  2. ② The exercise of the rights under Item 1 may be done through written, e-mail or FAX according to Enforcement Ordinance Attached Sheet No. 8 of the Enforcement Regulations of the Personal Information Protection Act for The homepage. The homepage will take action without delay.
  3. ③ If the information subject has requested correction or deletion of personal information, NILE will not use or provide the personal information until the correction or deletion is completed.
  4. ④ The exercise of the rights under Item 1 may be done through the legal representative of the information subject or the agent who has been delegated. In this case, you must submit a power of attorney according to Attached Sheet No. 11 of the Enforcement Regulations of the 「Personal Information Protection Act」.

② The information subject can decide whether to allow or not to collect information by cookies through the security policy of web browser. Article 8 (Destruction of Personal Information)

  1. ① If personal information becomes unnecessary, such as the elapse of the personal information retention period and achievement of the processing purpose, we will immediately destroy the personal information without any delay.
  2. ② If personal information has to be kept in accordance with other laws and regulations even though the period of holding personal information agreed with the information subject has elapsed or the purpose of processing has been accomplished, the personal information (or personal information file) may be stored in a separate database or we store it in a different storing place.
  3. ③ Procedures and methods of personal information destruction are as follows.
    • 1. Destruction procedure
      • - NILE destroys personal information by establishing a destruction plan for personal information (or personal information files) that should be destroyed.
      • - NILE shall select the personal information (or personal information file) that caused the reason for destruction, and destroy the personal information (or personal information file) with the approval of NILE's personal information protection chief officer.
    • 2. Destruction method
      • NILE destroys personal information recorded and stored in electronic file format so that it can not be played back. Personal information recorded or stored in paper documents is destroyed by burning or crushing through a crusher.
